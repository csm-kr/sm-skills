# Cover Background Quality Rule

Use this check whenever a deck has a cover or section-opening slide.

## Failure Mode

A cover can look washed out when these are combined:

- Pale paper background across the full slide.
- Low-opacity texture, ruled lines, or gradients behind headline text.
- White or ivory panels layered over a similar ivory background.
- Full-slide photo overlays with opacity, blur, or translucent white washes.

## Required Practice

- The cover must have one clearly dominant background treatment: solid color, real photo, or framed product image.
- If the background is pale, text must sit on a distinct solid panel or use high-contrast ink with no texture directly behind it.
- Do not place repeating-line paper texture behind the cover headline unless the rendered screenshot proves the contrast is strong.
- Do not use opacity overlays on cover imagery to "soften" the slide. Adjust crop, framing, or panel placement instead.
- Cover text contrast must be visually checked in the PDF contact sheet before delivery.

## QA Checklist

Before final delivery:

1. Render the PDF contact sheet.
2. Inspect slide 1 at thumbnail size and full size.
3. Confirm the cover does not look faded compared with the rest of the deck.
4. Confirm headline and subtitle remain readable without zooming.
5. If slide 1 looks washed out, replace the background with a solid panel or darker field and rerender.
